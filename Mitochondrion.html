Mitochondrion

Mitochondria are responsible for many biochemical mechanisms that are crucial for an organism's survival. The most notable is ATP synthesis. ATP is a nucleic acid that is used by many proteins as an activating agent, or in other words, an energy source. Besides ATP synthesis, mitochondria have important functions in other metabolic processes, such as fatty acid synthesis and amino acid catabolism. Mitochondria also has its own DNA and translates its own proteins, and is the only organelle besides the nucleus that does.
